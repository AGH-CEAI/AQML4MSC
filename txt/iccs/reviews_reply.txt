We thank the reviewer for the positive assessment of the novelty, clarity, and relevance of our work. 
We also appreciate the suggestion regarding the discussion of noise effects in quantum computations.

In the revised manuscript, we have added a discussion addressing the impact of noise on quantum models. 
Specifically, we clarify that all experiments were conducted using noiseless simulators to isolate the behavior of the proposed 
AQML framework. We also reference prior work (Rybotycki et al. 2025), which analyzes the effects of noise 
and real-device execution for similar PQC-based models on the ONERA dataset. Based on these findings, we expect that introducing 
noise would lead to performance degradation in our approach as well. A more comprehensive experimental evaluation under realistic noise conditions is an important direction 
for future work.

----------------------- REVIEW 3 ---------------------
We thank the reviewer for the detailed summary and constructive feedback. We are glad that the overall approach 
and its contribution are clearly understood and considered relevant.

Regarding the strength of the experimental evaluation, we would like to clarify two points:

1. Choice of classical baselines
We intentionally used a simple MLP as a baseline, as the primary goal of this work is to demonstrate the capability of AQML 
to discover effective quantum architectures, rather than to outperform state-of-the-art classical models. In addition, 
the synthetic MNIST-based dataset is a toy problem that does not require highly complex models to achieve strong performance. 
We have clarified this rationale in the revised manuscript. For the ONERA dataset, we also acknowledge that more advanced 
classical approaches can achieve higher accuracy(Daudt et al. 2018, Rybotycki et al. 2025), but they typically rely 
on significantly larger numbers of trainable parameters.

2. Noise and real-device experiments
We agree that evaluating performance under realistic noise conditions is important. In this work, we restricted experiments 
to noiseless simulators to focus on the architectural aspects of AQML. We have now added a discussion referencing prior 
work (Rybotycki et al. 2025), which studies the impact of noise and real quantum hardware for similar PQC-based approaches 
on the ONERA dataset. Based on these results, we expect a similar sensitivity to noise in our setting. 
Extending the evaluation to noisy simulators and real devices is part of our planned future work.

We have incorporated these clarifications into the manuscript to improve completeness and address the reviewer’s concerns.
