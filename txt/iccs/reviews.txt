----------- Comments for the authors -----------
The paper presents a clear study of automated quantum machine learning for multisource classification and data fusion. The comparison between classical baselines, manual PQC architectures, and AQML-generated circuits is well designed, and the experiments on both synthetic MNIST and the ONERA dataset demonstrate the potential of the approach. The results showing competitive accuracy with significantly fewer parameters are particularly interesting. Overall, this is a solid and well-presented contribution.

----------- Comments for the authors -----------
This paper explores the use of automated quantum machine learning (AQML) to design quantum circuits for multisource classification problems. The authors focus on feature-level data fusion, where information from multiple inputs is combined before classification. Their approach uses a hybrid architecture in which classical neural networks extract features from the inputs, while a parameterized quantum circuit acts as the classifier. The architecture of the quantum circuit is selected automatically using an AQML framework based on hyperparameter optimization.

The method is evaluated on two datasets: a synthetic multisource version of MNIST and the ONERA multispectral satellite dataset for change detection. The results show that the automatically generated quantum circuits perform better than manually designed quantum models and achieve results close to those of classical multilayer perceptrons while using far fewer trainable parameters. On the ONERA dataset, the approach also improves upon previously reported QML results.

Overall, the paper is clearly written and presents an interesting application of automated architecture search in quantum machine learning. However, the experimental evaluation could be stronger, particularly through comparisons with more advanced classical models and experiments on noisy or real quantum devices.
